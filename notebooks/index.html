While molecules are 3D objects and are typically drawn as 2.5D graphs (chirality is the 0.5), 2D string representations are useful in cheminformatics. Common methods for representing molecules as strings include SMILES and InChI. Other useful conventions to study include SMARTS for substructures and SMIRKS for reactions. The conformers of a molecule are key to its shape and functions. However, obtaining high-quality conformers can be computationally costly, requiring the use of a QM physics engine. To enable conformer generation on a greater scale, computational chemists have come up with many approximations and methods to quickly create reasonable conformers.
